TL;DR: 本文提出Checkerboard，一种无需学习、理论驱动的干净标签后门攻击方法，通过闭式推导的棋盘格触发器和复杂度驱动样本选择，在低投毒预算下实现SOTA攻击成功率（如CIFAR-10上20个样本可达99.99% ASR），且能抵抗多种先进防御。

Motivation: 现有干净标签后门攻击通常依赖昂贵的优化、代理模型训练或复杂数据访问，缺乏高效、简单的实现方案，因此需要一种理论上可行且实际高效的攻击方法。

Method: 1) 从线性可分性理论出发，闭式推导出棋盘格触发模式（Checkerboard trigger），无需代理模型训练或触发优化；2) 针对纹理丰富数据集，提出复杂度驱动的样本选择（Complexity-driven Sample Selection），仅使用目标类数据选择低复杂度图像提升触发-背景对比度。

Result: 在4个基准数据集上超越8种基线攻击，达到SOTA性能：CIFAR-10上20个样本（扰动预算10/255）达99.99% ASR；ImageNet-100上仅0.46%投毒率即达94%+ ASR且不降低干净准确率；能有效抵抗SOTA后门防御和自适应防御。

Conclusion: Checkerboard是一种高效、简单、理论驱动的干净标签后门攻击方法，在低投毒预算下表现优异且鲁棒性强，揭示了深度学习供应链中新的安全威胁。

Abstract: Backdoor attacks threaten the deep learning supply chain by poisoning a small fraction of the training data so that a model behaves normally on clean inputs but misclassifies trigger-carrying inputs to an attacker-chosen target class. Clean-label backdoor attacks are especially dangerous because poisoned samples remain label-consistent and are therefore harder to detect. Yet existing clean-label attacks typically rely on expensive optimization, surrogate-model training, or nontrivial data access.   We present Checkerboard, a theoretically grounded, learning-free clean-label backdoor attack that is effective, efficient, and simple to implement. From a linear separability formulation, we derive a checkerboard trigger in closed form, removing the need for surrogate-model training and trigger optimization. For texture-rich datasets, we introduce Complexity-driven Sample Selection, which uses only target-class data to improve trigger-to-background contrast by selecting low-complexity images for poisoning. Across four benchmark datasets, Checkerboard outperforms 8 baseline attacks and achieves state-of-the-art performance under low poisoning budgets. For example, on CIFAR-10, under a trigger perturbation budget of $10/255$, poisoning 20 training samples achieves $99.99\%$ Attack Success Rate (ASR). On ImageNet-100, a poisoning rate of only $0.46\%$ yields over $94\%$ ASR without degrading clean accuracy. The proposed attack also remains effective against state-of-the-art backdoor defenses and shows strong resistance to adaptive defenses.
